Vaccine Hesitancy and its Impact on the Spread of COVID-19 Cases

« 1 2 3 4 »


Scene 3: The line graph above displays the rolling averages of cases per 100K individuals across the 6 most vaccine-receptive states with the highest vaccination rates, using data provided by The New York Times. Users can toggle the button to see the development of COVID-19 cases in each of the six states ‐ Connecticut, Massachusetts, New Jersey, New York, Rhode Island, and Vermont ‐ since the beginning of the pandemic in February 2020.

Since vaccinations have become more accessible and prevalent since February 2021, especially since April, we can observe a huge drop in cases in all six states. On July 16, 2021, the total cases were: 39 in Connecticut, 68 in Massachusetts, 283 in New Jersey, 286 in New York, 27 in Rhode Island, and 9 in Vermont. The average cases per 100,000 individuals were: 1.19 in Connecticut, 1.29 in Massachusetts, 2.95 in New Jersey, 2.22 in New York, 2.43 in Rhode Island, and 1.1 in Vermont.

The data used for this graphic is publicly available and can be accessed at: https://github.com/nytimes/covid-19-data
